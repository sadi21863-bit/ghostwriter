# Scene 13 — Version A

**Prompt:** Write Mira's internal monologue as she admits to herself how far she's willing to go to find Priya.

**Word count:** 531

---

The phone pinged at the docks.

Six days dark and then a pulse, one pulse, like a heart that forgot to stay stopped. Could mean anything. Could mean nothing. Could mean she's there. Could mean someone wants me to think she's there.

Stop. Read it again. Pier 9, 2:14 a.m. Then gone.

Priya's number. Priya's phone. Not the same thing. Learned that lesson, didn't I. Always teaching me. Trust the metadata, never the meaning.

She'd say I'm doing it again. The face I make. She used to imitate it, the jaw, the way I go still. You go fridge-cold, she said. You go off somewhere I can't follow. And then I'd follow her instead, three years older and always the one who—

No.

The dock. Stay on the dock.

I have the badge that isn't a badge anymore. Have Reyes's number, which I shouldn't, which he'd answer at 2 a.m. and that's the problem, he'd answer. He always stands in the doorway. Hand on the frame. Mira, what are you going to do. As if asking it slows me down.

What am I willing to.

There. The thing. Say it.

The man at the records office. Tomorrow. Could lean on him, the gambling, the wife who doesn't know. Could. Would. Yes. Without the hesitation I'm pretending to have right now. Lean until he gives me Kane's logistics calendar and I don't feel the thing I'm supposed to feel about it.

That's new. The not-feeling. Or is it.

Kane's calm voice on the committee feed — we owe these decisions to people who understand the cost. Smooth. Silver. The cost. He means Priya is a cost. A line item buried under national. Under necessary. I will dig her out of his arithmetic if I have to break every—

Every what. Finish it.

Every person between me and the answer.

There. Now it's in the room.

Reyes thinks he's protecting me. From Kane. Not from. The other thing. The thing he saw in me at the end, when they pinned the leak and I went hunting for who actually did it and didn't care, genuinely did not care, who got cut on the way. He saw it. Stood in the doorway then too.

I told myself this is different. Family. This is righteous. But the hands are the same hands. The cold is the same cold. The not-feeling that lets me pick up the receiver and dial the records man's wife if I have to and let her hear just enough—

I would.

God. I would.

And if she's dead. If the dock is just a place a phone got dropped or planted and she's been gone gone since day one. What then. Then I do it anyway. Then it isn't even for her, it's for the burning. Admit that. The wanting to set it all alight.

Too late, the fear says. Already too late. Already the kind of person who—

The phone pinged at the docks.

Start there. Hold the one true thing. Pier 9, 2:14.

Going to the docks. Going to do whatever the docks ask of me.

Don't tell Reyes. He'd stand in the doorway.

I'd go through him too.
