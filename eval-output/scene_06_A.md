# Scene 06 — Version A

**Prompt:** Write an atmospheric opening scene set in the abandoned dock warehouse at night, before anything happens.

**Word count:** 361

---

The harbor wind found its way in first, threading through the gaps in the corrugated walls so the whole structure breathed—a low metal moan that rose and fell, rose and fell, as if the warehouse were testing the air for something. Sheet metal ticked overhead where it had worked loose from its rivets. Somewhere in the dark a chain swung against a support beam, and the sound came in slow intervals, a clock that kept no honest time.

The smell reached deeper than the cold did. Brine, first—that mineral sharpness of seawater gone stagnant in the channel outside, kelp rotting against the pilings. Beneath it, the thick sweetness of diesel soaked into concrete decades ago, never fully gone. And under that, fainter, the green-black note of mildew blooming in the rope coils stacked along the far wall, a smell that pressed against the back of the throat and pulled something loose from much further back—the underside of a childhood pier, maybe, or the inside of a coat that had been left out in the rain.

Marlow felt his shoulders drop before he understood why. It was the dark that did it, the way it asked nothing of him. Then the moan of the wind shifted pitch and his spine went straight again, every cheap nerve he had wiring back to alertness. The body knew this place was wrong before the mind bothered with the case for it. He stood very still inside the doorway and let his pulse decide.

Water moved somewhere below the floorboards—the slow suck and slap of the tide working at the foundations, patient, indifferent. It had been doing that long before anyone built here. It would keep doing it after. The sound did not care that he had come. That was almost a comfort, until the chain swung again and struck the beam, and the note hung in the cold a beat too long, as though the air itself were waiting to see what he would do.

He breathed in the diesel and the rot and the salt. He waited with the building. Outside, a gull cried once over the black water and did not cry again.
