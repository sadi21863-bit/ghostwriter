# Scene 18 — Version B

**Prompt:** Write a darkly funny exchange between Mira and Daniel while they're patching up a wound, to relieve the tension.

**Word count:** 380

---

The bullet had gone clean through the meat of her shoulder, which Daniel kept saying like it was good news.

"Clean through," he said again, threading the irrigation syringe. "You should be thanking the universe."

"I'll send a card." Mira braced her good hand against the gas station bathroom sink, watching herself go gray in the mirror. "What's the address for the universe."

"You're not funny when you're bleeding."

"I'm not funny when I'm not bleeding. That's the tragedy."

He flushed the wound. She made a sound that was mostly air and the rest profanity, and the back of her head cracked against the tile.

"Hold still."

"I'm holding still. The wall moved."

Daniel ripped the packaging on the gauze with his teeth, hands occupied, and started packing it in. "Okay. This is going to feel like the worst thing that's ever happened to you."

"You've met my career."

"Second worst, then." He pressed. She inhaled through her nose, slow, the way the agency taught you to breathe under interrogation, which was the only useful thing the agency ever taught her and she was now using it in a Sunoco. "You know what they don't tell you in the field manual?"

"Surprise me."

"That the standard trauma kit doesn't come with anything for the screaming." He taped the edge down. "It's got tourniquets. Hemostatic powder. Chest seals. No earplugs."

"Maybe I should requisition some."

"You don't have a job."

"That's the perfect time to requisition. No oversight."

He sat back on his heels, blood to the wrists, and looked at her in a way she didn't like because it was the way he looked at her right before he said something true. "You know if you'd told me where you were going—"

"You'd have stood in front of the door." She rolled the shoulder a half inch and regretted all of it. "You always stand in front of the door, Daniel. You're a very expensive doorstop."

"And you'd still have gone out the window."

"And I'd have made it out the window cleanly. That's the difference. You made me rush it." She nodded at the shoulder. "This is technically your fault."

"I wasn't there."

"Negligence." She tested a smile and it mostly held. "I'm going to requisition a lawsuit too."
